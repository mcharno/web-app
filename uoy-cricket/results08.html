Thur. 3rd July PDP XI  87 A.O

The by now traditional 4pm downpour came at 4pm. Is this Florida? But unlike last week the short burst was all there was and all those sun dances paid off as the defending champs lost the toss and took the field to ball at one of this seasons highfliers PDP.  Conditions were good for bowling, it was muggy and hopes were that it would swing and it did as Groker unleashed a devasting opening spell of 4 overs taking 3 wickets for 1 & the 1 came courtesy off a very arguable wide.  The coup de grace of Croker's haul being a last ball slow one to take the last of his 3 wickets. PDP slumping to 10 for 3 & it was looking ominous already as Captain Holmes rang the changes with the bowling (controversially taking off crowd favourite Levesley after only two overs despite him bowling a maiden and Holmes himself dropping a "sharpish" chance at slip off his bowling). The good work of Croker was to continue as Srihdar (3) and then Amir (2) took 5 more wickets, Shirdah at one point been on a hat-trick. Pandey 14 and Vishal 28 did their best to repair the damage of those early Croker overs, but PDP would slump to 87 A.O. 4 of the wickets came courtesy of Staff league legend Mendham who put on a master class of wicket keeping to take 4 stumpings.  

So 88 to get. On the face of it a small total to defend and you had to fancy the Panther's chances. But PDP were having none of it! They sent early shockwaves through the Panthers batting line-up and suddenly got the crowd buzzing with interest at a potential shock in the making as they reduced the Panthers to 3-10 in the first 2 overs as opener McIntosh went for a duck and then bowling hero Croker was ran out in a mix-up with partner Cooksey. Memories of Boycott-Randell circa 77 came flooding back as Croker came trudging back to the side lines. Could Cooksey make amends? Suddenly 88 was looking a long way off. Cometh the hour, cometh the man as Panther's warshorse Mendham strode to the wicket and effectively turned the game back in the Panther's favour with a quick fire 34 off only 18 balls including a massive 6 over deep mid on. A crucial innings and as he lost his wicket going for yet another big 6, Vishan catching him close to the boundary, it was left to Holmes (18*) and the redeemed Cooksey  (30*)  to reach the target in 11.1 overs. PDP giving the Panthers a real scare. 


So Panthers go top (for 24 hrs at least) and its over to Bios as the league waits for  next week's massive encounter.

Thur. 10th July Bioaccumulators XI 92 A.O Say one thing about the cricket deities, they have a sense of drama! This game would be played out under  a steel sky with thunder clouds overhead. An appropo setting for what would turn into an epic! (like this match report). Tension was high for this match and the waiting crowd (complete with barbie (only in England would you play cricket in this weather with the crowd having a barbie)) sensed that this might well be a match to savour. Bios lost toss and (10 man) Panther's Captain Holmes inserted them on the third strip. Staff league stalwarts will know the one; undulates like corrigated iron and makes the leg side boundary very short. The undulation was made worse by a green top and a very low bounce. Even before the match started you knew this was going to be a low scoring affair.

Aziz & Davis (6) opened for Bios. Aziz (9) looking in good nick, hitting a massive 6 before Hodgson (just arrived back from Coz that day) ran him out with a direct hit from covers.  This brought Wilson, who played a stalwart innings defying everything the Pather's threw at him for his 11, to the crease. Aamir took Davis' wicket to reduce Bios to 23 for 2 in the 4th over. Enter Affaq. Such has been Affaq's form this season in Staff  and Vale league that his wicket is probably the most coveted wicket of 2008. This was underlined by the fact the Panther's brought on their big guns of Hodgson & Holmes at almost the earliest opportunity to try and take it. Affaq signalling his intent with a 4 whipped through the leg side off his first ball & then clobbering Hodgson's first delivery for 6. Game on! Panthers throw the proverbial at Affaq to try and dislodge him. The only sniff being a very sharp chance off Holmes from a full blooded drive to Hodgson at mid on that dipped at his feet. 6 overs passed as Affaq & Wilson took the score on to 60 and it looked like The Panthers could be facing their first 100+ total of the season. Then it happened. Affaq hit a ball through mid-on, Gul at third man came across, Affaq decided to chance it on Gul's arm coming through for a 2nd. Gul span round and pinged it in from just short of the boundary. Direct hit! Affaq ran out 27. Cue mad celebrations as The Panthers swamp Gul. On the face of it an unnecessary risk & you really have to wonder just how important that run out will be.  With Affaq gone you sensed any hope of 100+ had gone to and  so it proved as Sridhar mopped up the middle order with his second consecutive 3 for ( 3-15 off 4), Supported by Holmes & Hodgson. Holmes finally dismissing Wilson who hung in valiantly. So Panthers become the first team to dismiss Bios all season as Bios set a target of 92.

Normally the Panthers would be confident of knocking off a sub-100 total. But the very fact that a powerful batting line up like Bios hadn't managed it, showed that this would be a tough ask on this low bouncing wicket & it was. The next 19.4 overs would put both sets of players through the wringer as fortunes swung back and forth. Bios open bowling with Hodgkinson (taken off after 1 over - more on the next three later!) and Birnie. Panthers getting off to a slow start making only 22 off the first 5 overs as they lost opener McIntosh for 2 and last week's hero Cooksey for 3. As Bernie and Aziz send rumbles through the crowd. Pressure on. The big guns of the Panthers attack were still there though and so you had to fancy The Panthers with Hodgson still at the crease and Holmes & Mendham still waiting in the wings. Gul joined Hodgson at the wicket and set about repairing the damage of the early overs. It was slow going as some very tight Bios bowling, combined with sharp fielding and the low bouncing track making scoring very difficult. After 11 overs the Panthers had 49. Still 44 short of the desired target, but with wickets in hands. 12th over. Cue Mayhem! Affaq brought into the attack, Gul nudges a single, Hodgson plays out a dot, takes a big stride down the wicket to hit the next delivery. Bios go up. LBW! Suddenly Bios were right back in it. Out strode Holmes. On the boundary, the rugby balls and footballs suddenly stopped being kicked as all attention turned to the pitch.  If Holmes could see out Affaq then a few lusty blows and the match could well be over.  Hodgkinson would turn that on its heads thank you very much. First Aziz removed Gul for 12 as Gul tried to hit out in an attempt to push the strike rate along. Panthers 58-4 off 13. By now Mendham had joined Holmes at the wicket.  These two have won countless matches for The Panthers and whilst they were there you just had to fancy The Panthers to win. Hodgkinson was brought back and would send Bios and their fans into wild celebrations as he bowled Holmes with his first delivery and then followed it the very next delivery by taking a Sridhar's wicket for a golden duck. Hat trick ball.  It missed Aamir's edge by a whisker. Suddenly the Panthers were in real trouble. 63-6 with only two bonafide rabbits left in the hutch and still 30 more runs to get iff 5 overs! Could this be it? But whilst Mendham was there you know that it only takes him one big over and the match could swing again. But the pendulum wa svery much with Bios now. Some astute hitting by Mendham took the score to 75 off 17. 18th over, Affaq's last. Mendham hits a four, the batsmen share a few singles. Last delivery. Panthers only need 10 with 3.1 overs left. Mendham still there. Panthers favourites again. Last ball. WICKET! Mendham gone for 12 caught on the leg side. Bios and their fans go wild. Big swing back towards the Bios. So 10 needed for the win. Only Aamir, Everitt & Levesley (who doesn't know one end of a bat from the other) left. Destroyer in chief Hodgkinson comes back for his last over with figures of 8-2. Two leg side deliveries are spanked to the boundary by Aamir. Panthers now going wild as 1 more run and its at least a tie! Next delivery is nudged to the leg side and the scores are all square! Panthers assured of at least 1 point. 4th delivery of 19th over. Everitt nudges it through covers & ambles down the wicket to take the winning runs! Panthers erupt!

What a game! Both teams going at it hammer and tong  in a game that swung back and forth. Excellent cricket.

So Panthers go back to the top and their unbeaten run is extended (by the narrowest of margins). Next up  Alcuin. Bios have a week off  beofre they next face Alcos. Will Alcos pay for this defeat?
