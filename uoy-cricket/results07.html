Details now flooding in about the Psycho-Alcos, surely a pun in there somewhere, friendly. After using all means fair and some foul, Psychos managed to cobble together 8 players to take the field. A friend of the team tells me that one of the players was taught the rudiments of the game by been sat in front of wikipedia for a hour before the match!  Alco batted first and gave Psychos 3 fielders to bolster their numbers.  In an equally sporting gesture, Alcos reversed their batting order and managed to compile 98 off their 20 overs.  Only 7 of the Psychos were due to bat and the loss of 3 wickets for 0 didn't help the cause. But Prendegast once again almost came to the rescue, sharing a 67 run partnership with Morland for the 5th wicket. With 18 needed off the last 3 and Prendegast still there it looked like Psychos might just pull it off. But he fell shortly after and the unlikely (friendly) victory was not to be.

A complete contrast to the previous GSA match with wickets falling like 9 pins (a mixed metaphor if ever there was one). 

GSA won toss and elected to bat on a gloriously sunny evening. Though the match did start well after 5:30 due to late arrival of some players depsite Dave's warning of the early onset of darkness. Teams really must try to get games going at 5:30. Its dark at 8 now and the ball is basically lost from a fielder's view, never mind a batsmen after 8.  GSA mixed up their batting order for this game to allow some of the guys who had little to no batting this season a chance to have a dart resulting in a 6,7 & 8 of Mumtaz, Holmes & Mendham. Quite a powerful lower mid order on paper. Who would have expetced what was the follow? The Panthers went off like the proverbial, rattling along at over 10 an over for the first 10 overs.  A big score was on the cards. But credit to the Alcuin bowlers as they took wickets at regular intervals to staunch the run flow of the GSA.  Abbas & Walker starring with the ball. Abbas returning figures of 3 -32.  Removing Levesley & Fulcher, in their first innings of the season, for ducks in 3 balls.  Headlines also to Walker who took the slightly more covetted pairing of Holmes and Mendham. Walker picking up Holmes, last seasons star batter, for his first duck in staff league cricket! Its not often that I can statistically say I am up their with Holmes with the bat! Mutaz carried on his personal crusade to lose balls with a rapid 13 including two 6s on the trot, in a season total of 13, before falling to Shehzad.  The first 5 overs of the innings had brought 54 runs for the loss of 1 wicket. The next 5, 52 for the loss of 5.  Even though the run-rate was above 10 an over. The clutter of wickets effectively stopped GSA matching their final game perfomance of the 2006 season. One constant throughout the early part of the innings was Kamran, promoted to number 3 for this match, who played a sparkling innings of 66 at more than 1.5 runs per ball.  When he fell this brought Chemist Vesey to the wicket to support McIntosh as the last 10 over brought 73 runs for the loss of only 2 wickets.  Both notching not outs, Vesey 13 & McIntosh 36.  GSA finishing with 173 - 8. Dave will have to confirm, but I suspect that the GSA season total of 1266 runs is a new league record for runs in a season. 

After official team photo (soon to appear in glorious technicolour on the website)  of the 2007 champs was taken. Alcuin took the field. The late start meaning that the last few overs would be increasingly difficult for both batters and fielders. As with the batting, GSA mixed up their attack. Making it a nightmare for the poor Alcuin scorer. In short the Panthers used all 10 bowlers in rotation with each bowler only getting a 1 over spell. As the usual scorer for the GSA I tip my cap to the Alcuin scorer who managed to keep track so well of the constant changes. The innings was underpinned by Abbas, who capped a fine all round perfomance with a 70*. It must be difficult for a batter to face a constantly changing bowling line up and the fact that Abbas hardly gave a chance is a testament to the skill of the innings.  At the other end no one could really stay with him as wicket tumbled at fairly regular intervals. Holmes taking 3 for 0 in his 2 over spell which stared in over 2 and ended in over 12, as the pick of the bowlers. Chemist Vesey taking a wicket with a "Flintoff" also worthy of mention.  At the end of the innings it became increasingly difficult to field the ball. An outfielder basically only seeing the ball when it was a yard away from them.  If these late finishes to the season are to carry on in the future then teams really have to get going at 5:30 spot on. 

So the Panthers extend their unbeaten run to a record 19 games and become only the second team to go through two seasons unbeaten. Dave informs me it occurred in the 80s, but the league was much smaller then. Most of this year's Panthers team is around for 2008, so it looks likely that the Panthers will be there or there abouts in 2008, looking to join a very exclusive club that has only Chemistry as a member: the 3-peaters! 

Alcuin have a mixed season. Notching a string of 100+ scores but only winning 2 games. Suggestive that the bowling attack needs a little strengthening to make them truly competive.  100+ scores are basically only 1 batsmen short of being defendable so if the bowling can be improved and the batting strengthed this would make Alcuin a dangerous floater for other teams.

 "Its left to Chemistry and S&N to play out the final part of this soap opera that we call staff league.  Can Chemistry finish with a win to secure yet another top 3 placing or will S&N spring a surprise?" The question that was asked of this final encounter between the team to suffer the most from   the fickle finger of the weather deities and the sun-blessed goodness that is S&N, the only team not to have a  game cancelled in this wettest of british summers (thanks to the Met Office for that snippet of info!).  The rage that Chemistry must have internalised as their pursuit of a 15th title was hampered by 5 lost games must have been released in a cathartic cleansing of the soul as they summer-ily trounced the Science Park Boys. 

S&N bat first and can muster only 79. Not a bad score against one of the league's best bowling attackes. But in this new era of powerhouse batting never going to be enough to defend. Duckett, Burns & Nomi take a lesson out of the Jose Mourinho book and cash in with a 4-3-3 (respectively) haul.  Lanton offering some resistance with all the threes, 33. 

In reply Chemistry cruise to 80-2 with Anant notching 27. To complete the victory that sees Chemistry take up yet another top 3 position. 

S&N finish the season in 9th. With a mixed bag of a year really. A trait common to most of the teams in the lower half of the league. High scores dotted about and some good bowling performances. But not enough in either department to really challenge the top 4 or 5 teams. Though a highlight must be the Psychos game where they took the leagues surprise package to the tape!  3+ high scores suggest the batting is sound, but maybe its the bowling that needs a  little more zest in 2008.

What can one say about the league's Liverpool? Record title winners, who are now in their (equal) longest barren spell. Perhaps unlucky to meet a very fired-up Panthers in the first match they played this season and then definitely unlucky to lose 5 games to the weather. Obviously still one of the major forces in staff league cricket & will be looking forward to 2008 for a chance to regain a trophy that they have held aloft more times than all the other teams combined, though its likely they will have to replace Parthi, a backbone and star performer over the last 4 seasons.
