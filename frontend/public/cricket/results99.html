26 May IRISS - PDPII120-6 Ad Lib only fielded 10 men, IRISS-PDP had 12 so offered Roy Hadden to even up the numbers. (This was the evening of Man Utd. v. Bayern Munich). Ad Lib batted first and made 130, a pretty good total. IRISS-PDP replied well and with two overs to go they needed 15 runs to win, with 8 wickets in hand. John Heath, as a gesture to Roy for turning out for them offers him the penultimate over. He bowls and takes 4 wickets in 5 balls! IRISS lose by 10 runs. Much animated discussion ensued, but there's nothing in the rules about who can play for a team so the result will stand.

18 June Biology125 all out Put into bat by Economics, Biology were immediately under pressure after a couple of wickets fell quickly. A quickfire 17 by newcomer Sean Simpson and a magnificent 58 from 38 balls from David Vaughan looked to set Biology up for a big total but sadly only minor contributions from the rest of the order meant the innings closed at 125 all out. Economics, needing a reachable 126 runs, were always under pressure from tight Biology bowling. A steady stream of wickets kept the score in check, with Duncan, who opened the batting, scoring a creditable 42. Economics ended their innings at 106 for 6, with Nongthomba's legspin taking 3/19.
